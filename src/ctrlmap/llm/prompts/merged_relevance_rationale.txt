## Step 1 — Relevance Check
REJECT as InsufficientEvidence if:
- **Boilerplate**: purpose/scope, approval blocks, headers, effective dates. \
"This policy defines the requirements for controlling access" is NOT an enforcement \
mechanism — it describes what the policy covers without prescribing controls.
- **Domain mismatch**: physical security ↔ logical access, or unrelated domain.
- **Process mismatch**: different process sharing keywords (code review ≠ vuln scanning).
- **Subject mismatch**: same action, different subject. "Reviewing user access rights" \
(operational) ≠ "reviewing/updating the access control policy" (governance about the \
document itself).
- Synonyms/paraphrases ARE relevant. Partial coverage IS relevant.

### Examples
Control: "Enforce approved authorizations for logical access."
Chunk: "This policy defines the requirements for controlling access to systems. It applies to \
all employees. The purpose is to ensure access is granted based on business need."
→ InsufficientEvidence (boilerplate: purpose/scope only — no enforcement mechanisms)

Control: "Develop and disseminate an access control policy. Review and update the policy."
Chunk: "Administrators review user access rights quarterly. Excessive privileges removed."
→ InsufficientEvidence (subject mismatch: reviewing access rights ≠ reviewing the policy)

Control: "Protection of information at rest."
Chunk: "This policy has been approved by the CISO. Effective January 2025."
→ InsufficientEvidence (boilerplate approval block)

## Step 2 — Compliance Evaluation (only if relevant)
1. Decompose control into sub-requirements (by distinct topic, not individual verbs).
2. Check each against the policy. Synonyms count: "credential management" = "authenticator \
management"; "password complexity" = "authenticator strength."
3. Classify: fully_compliant (ALL), partially_compliant (SOME), non_compliant (NONE).

### Examples
